Dr Kwame Nkrumah

The man who lead Ghana to obtain Independence.

Dr Kwame Nkrumah giving a speech.

Here's a time line of Dr. Nkrumah's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Nkroful, Gold Coast (now Ghana)
  • 1935 - Receives a scholarship to study at the Lincoln University in Pennsylvania, United States
  • 1939 - Completes his Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics and Sociology, and later obtains a Bachelor of Theology degree from the Lincoln Theological Seminary
  • 1945 - Earns a Master's degree in Education from the University of Pennsylvania
  • 1947 - Returns to the Gold Coast and becomes involved in the nationalist movement
  • 1949 - Forms the Convention People's Party (CPP), advocating for independence from British colonial rule
  • 1951 - Leads the CPP to victory in the general elections, becoming the Prime Minister of the Gold Coast
  • 1957 - Gold Coast gains independence from British colonial rule and is renamed Ghana; Nkrumah becomes the Prime Minister of Ghana
  • 1960 - Ghana becomes a republic, and Nkrumah becomes the country's first President
  • 1963 - Establishes the Organization of African Unity (OAU), aimed at promoting unity and cooperation among African nations
  • 1964 - Declares Ghana a one-party state and assumes the title of "President for Life"
  • 1966 - Ousted from power in a military coup while he is on a state visit to China
  • 1972 - Lives in exile in Guinea, continuing his political activism and writing books
  • 1978 - Passes away in Bucharest, Romania, while receiving medical treatment

Nkrumah's legacy as a pan-Africanist and champion of African independence continues to inspire leaders and activists across the continent. His vision for a united and prosperous Africa remains an important part of his enduring legacy.
